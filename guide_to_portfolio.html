User, Integrator and Developer Help

It is always fun to learn new technogies, and my main focus is on creating content and structures that provide exceptional user experiences, but it is highly gratifying to get feedback that validates the content's contribution to the success of the product and company.

At Intel, the command line for three technical computing analyzer software products was identified as a potential barrier to customer adoption and therefore a documentation priority. Testing where possible, I cleaned up old content that did not adequately explain product behavior, addressed some common issues mentioned by users in the developer forums, and added some cookbook-style documentation that was much requested by internal customers. When I updated the Static Analysis Getting Started Guide, I chose sample problems that illustrated more interesting product capabilities and added important productivity tips to enhance the user’s first experience. 

At Kavi, more than one customer mentioned the help as one of the reasons they choose Kavi collaboration tools for standards organizations, including NISO and SMPTE. 

The Director of Marketing at Sight & Sound Software (later acquired by Datalex) credited the help as a key factor in the landing of a major contract.

Database

Studied object-oriented database design and programming at HSU, so I’m familiar with database diagrams, notations and SQL. Managed inventory and customer databases for a small company. Later documented databases for Datalex, a proprietary database for a custom web travel portal and a Microsoft Word template with macros for use by database designers and administrators. Created OO database designs for ICC and trained the office manager on the basics of OO theory and how to implement these designs.

At Project: WorldWide, created a series of hierarchy diagrams to assist Project Manager and BA with visualization of roles and permissions.  

XML

Documented XML first at Sight & Sound (Datalex). This included extensive information on the document model.

At ICC, where I was hired as an expert on XML markup before promotion to Systems Manager, my in-house training program on XML and liaison to technical leads gained the company additional work, including reconsideration for a large contract.
